Adjusted Coverage vs Reported Coverage
Classification Methodology
Fortress
Cash, cash equivalents, US Treasury & government securities, money market instruments. These are the most liquid and certain-value assets — they cannot be mispriced.
Excellent
First lien senior secured to defensive, non-AI-threatened industries: healthcare, insurance, aerospace & defense, utilities, infrastructure, food & beverage, chemicals, packaging, transportation, capital goods, manufacturing. Marked within 5% of par. No or minimal PIK (<2%). Also includes first lien to neutral sectors (financial services, professional services) with good marks.
Medium
First lien to cyclical or AI-exposed sectors (software, media, retail, staffing, education). Second lien to any sector with marks above -10%. First lien with no industry label (quality unverifiable). Moderate PIK (2-5%). Positions with MTM -5% to -15%.
Poor
Equity, preferred equity, JV interests, unsecured/subordinated debt. Heavy PIK (>5%). Severe markdowns (MTM < -15%). Any non-accrual or workout position. These are the assets most at risk of being left behind in a cherry-picking scenario.
Adjusted Coverage = (Total Assets − Poor-tier Fair Value) / Total Debt × 100. This shows the coverage ratio if all poor-quality assets were written to zero — a conservative floor for what BDC debt holders can rely on.

About — BDC Portfolio Analytics
Why This Exists

Business Development Companies file quarterly Schedules of Investments with the SEC — thousands of line items per BDC, each with fair value, cost, spread, lien position, and industry. This data is public but scattered across hundreds of XBRL filings in inconsistent formats.

This dashboard aggregates, normalizes, and cross-references that data so you can analyze individual BDC portfolios, compare marks across lenders, stress-test leverage scenarios, and track company-level credit quality over time — all from a single static page with no backend dependency.

Data Pipeline
1
SEC Filing Extraction — Schedule-of-investments data is parsed from 10-K and 10-Q XBRL filings via automated scripts that handle the wide variation in BDC disclosure formats (hierarchical, alternating-row, inline headers).
2
Normalization — Company names, investment types, lien positions, and rate structures are cleaned and standardized so that filings with very different layouts can be analyzed together within one consistent schema.
3
Cross-BDC Matching — Company aliases are resolved across BDCs so that overlapping holdings (e.g., “Acme Corp” in OBDC vs “Acme Corporation” in ARCC) are recognized as the same borrower. Sponsor mappings link portfolio companies to their PE owners.
4
Enrichment & Audit — Implied spreads are computed using actual FRED SOFR monthly averages. Every value is paired with a source link back to the SEC filing for verification. Balance sheet data and capital stack analysis are derived from supplemental XBRL facts.
Limitations & Verification

Fair values are management estimates. BDCs mark their own books — the “fair value” of an illiquid private credit instrument is a Level 3 estimate, not a market-clearing price. Cross-BDC mark comparisons reveal where managers disagree, but neither mark is necessarily “right.”

Filing-level source material remains the best reference point. Every data point in this dashboard traces back to an SEC filing. Use the source links ([src] icons throughout) to verify any number that matters to your analysis. Automated parsing handles thousands of line items but edge cases exist.

Industry classifications are approximate. BDCs use inconsistent industry labels. We normalize these but some borderline classifications (e.g., “Healthcare IT” as Software vs Healthcare) require judgment calls. The industry breakdown should be treated as directional, not definitive.

Technical Notes

Spread duration is computed as the annuity factor (1−(1+y)−T)/y at the all-in yield. For floating-rate loans, rate duration ≈ 0.25yr (quarterly reset) but spread duration ≈ 2–4yr because the spread is contractually fixed.

Implied spreads use actual SOFR monthly averages from FRED for the filing period, not a fixed assumption. This makes cross-period comparisons more accurate.

Stress test mechanics: For each +100bps of spread widening, fair value declines by approximately (spread_duration × 0.01) of par. Results show both gross (asset-level) and levered (NAV/equity) impact. Asset coverage ratio breach warnings trigger at the 150% BDC regulatory threshold.

Architecture: Single static HTML file (~13K lines), no backend, no build step. Data is pre-computed JSON fetched at load time. All charts use Chart.js; network graph uses D3.js force simulation.
